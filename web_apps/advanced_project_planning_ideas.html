Make plan claims testable

A completed activity is not the same as current evidence; evidence is not the same as a supported claim; and a supported claim is not an approved gate.

The checker reports the weakest link in each chain. It does not certify GDPR compliance, safety, operational readiness or any other external standard.

Allocate scarce expertise by opportunity cost

Teams disclose the outcome unlocked, cost of waiting, confidence in that claim, minimum useful package and a credible alternative. The protocol allocates days, not fictional money.

Priority is a transparent scenario score: delay cost × evidence confidence, adjusted for reuse and whether another route exists. Change the inputs and challenge the recommendation.

Inspect typed, directed dependencies

Precedence, evidence, approval and feedback links mean different things. A directed precedence cycle makes a schedule infeasible; a feedback loop may be useful learning; a second evidence path may be resilience.

The model tests reachability and directed precedence cycles. It does not reduce programme health to one graph statistic.

Run a transparent planner tournament

Five declared policies schedule the same dependency-constrained work under three plausible duration scenarios. The app compares consequences; none of the policies is “meta-learned”.

Lower regret is better. It measures how far a policy’s scenario score is from the best policy in that same scenario. Inputs and scoring rules are illustrative and inspectable.
